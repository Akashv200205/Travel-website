The Carnival of Recife and Olinda

One of the most vibrant and authentic celebrations in Brazil, blending music, dance, and culture in a unique way.

  1. Immerse Yourself in Frevo and Maracatu

    2. Recife’s Carnival is famous for frevo and maracatu, two energetic rhythms that take over the streets. Be sure to join the local blocos (street parties) and parades, where you can dance along to these electrifying beats. You can also take frevo lessons beforehand—an excellent way to get into the spirit!

  2. Parade with Olinda’s Giant Puppets

    3. In Olinda, the iconic Giant Puppets Parade is one of the festival’s highlights. These massive figures fill the streets in a dazzling display of color and joy. The town's cobbled streets and colonial architecture provide a beautiful backdrop to this enchanting event.

  3. Explore the Handicraft Markets

    4. Take home a piece of the Carnival spirit by visiting the local craft markets. You’ll find handmade items like vibrant masks, accessories, and custom Carnival shirts. These markets are a great way to support local artisans and discover Pernambuco’s creative culture.
